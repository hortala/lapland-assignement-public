General informations

Finland is a North European nation bordering Sweden, Norway and Russia. Its capital, Helsinki, occupies a peninsula and the surrounding islands of the Baltic Sea. It is home to the 18th-century Suomenlinna Maritime Fortress, the trendy Design District and various museums. The province of Arctic Lapland, a vast wilderness of national parks and ski resorts, is the perfect place to see the northern lights.

Capital city

In southern Finland, the capital Helsinki occupies a peninsula in the Gulf of Finland.

Its central avenue, Mannerheimintie, includes various institutions including the national museum that traces the history of the country, from the Stone Age to the present day. It also has the imposing Parliament and the Museum of Contemporary Art Kiasma. All of red brick, richly decorated, Ouspenski Cathedral overlooks the harbor.

National parks

There are no less than 39 national parks in Finland, and a fortieth park to be inaugurated in 2017 in honor of the centenary of our country's independence. These areas of unspoiled nature are spread over our entire territory, between the archipelago, lakes and woodlands, peat bogs or the area of the mountains of Lapland. Each of the Finnish national parks illustrates the natural features of the corresponding region, such as rapids, collapse ditches and sandy isthmuses.

Seasons

Finland is rich in all its exciting contrasts, between its four well-differentiated seasons, the midnight sun and winter darkness, the city and countryside, or the east and west of the country.

REASONS TO VISIT LAPLAND

Santa

Santa is also known and loved around the world. But did you know that he was born in Finland and that you can meet him in person? Children of all ages are welcome to visit Santa Claus' office in Rovaniemi every day of the year and to be received in private audience for an exchange of a few words with the friendly old man in red while immersing himself in a unique enchanted atmosphere. As everyone knows, Santa's mission is to bring happiness every year to the four corners of the planet with the help of his faithful fur companions reindeer.

Le rituel du sauna

If you want to understand Finland and its people, getting started in the sauna is a good starting point. The sauna is so integral to Finnish culture that it ranks at the top of our national symbols.

With its seawater pool, the Allas Sea Pool sports and relaxation complex is based on a new concept inspired by the spa tradition that has marked the history of the Baltic coast. You will be able to discover new sauna experiences all year round both in front of the sea and in the middle of a relaxed urban environment.

The Löyly Design Sauna, which is located not far from the city center at the southern end of the Helsinki peninsula, was recently inaugurated. This contemporary seaside resort also features a restaurant offering dishes that are representative of the finest Finnish cuisine, while the hotel's wooden building and its modern design are well worth the visit.

Silence and relaxation

In the hustle and bustle of today's life, what is most valuable is what is the rarest, in this case the vast spaces nothing for oneself, peace and serenity , the feeling of having time. Have all the room you need to breathe, all the time you need to dream. These precious treasures await you in Finland, where the lakes are numerous and the inhabitants much less.

A handful of pristine nature, a pinch of Finnish sauna, a spoonful of traditional health and relaxation treatments from home, a good portion of wild ingredients and some moderate physical activities in addition, this is the exclusive recipe that we propose for you. to assure you an absolutely total relaxation.

Auroras

Auroras

The magic of the aurora borealis show is for many visitors one of the strongest moments of their existence. These celestial phenomena are observed in Finnish Lapland roughly 200 nights a year, or about one night out of two provided the sky is clear. To really make the most of these luminous spurts, it is recommended to wait until it is dark and to choose an observation site far from any source of light pollution, for example streetlights and neon lights in the city.

This year, you will be able to further enhance your Northern Lights experience with the opening of various accommodation sites in Northern Finland, designed for enhanced observation of the northern sky and the spectacular light events it hosts. the desired effect is that the observer has the impression of floating in the atmosphere. Discover the TreeHouse Hotel in Rovaniemi, the Aurora Dome in Muonio and the Glass Villas in Kemi.
